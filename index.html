MIND BODY MEDICINE

Mind-body medicine is an approach 
to healthcare that recognizes the 
interconnectedness of the mind and 
body in overall health and wellness. 
It encompasses a range of practices 
and techniques aimed at promoting physical 
and emotional well-being by addressing 
the connections between the two.
Mind-body medicine techniques may 
include meditation, yoga, tai chi, 
deep breathing exercises, biofeedback, hypnosis, 
and cognitive-behavioral therapy, among 
others. These techniques aim to reduce 
stress, anxiety, and other negative 
emotions that can contribute to 
physical health problems.

PSYCHOTHERAPY

Psychotherapy is a form of treatment 
for mental health issues that involves 
talking with a trained mental health 
professional. It can be used to treat 
a range of mental health conditions, 
including anxiety, depression, and 
post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), 
among others. There are many different 
types of psychotherapy, each with its 
own approach and techniques:
(1)Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT)
(2)Psychodynamic therapy
(3)Humanistic therapy
(4)Family therapy
(5)Group therapy

Frequently Asked Questions

Therapy sessions typically last between 45 minutes to an hour, but the exact duration can be discussed and adjusted based on your needs and preferences.

Confidentiality is a crucial aspect of therapy. As a psychotherapist, I am legally and ethically bound to maintain the confidentiality of our sessions. However, there are some exceptions where I may be required to disclose information, such as if there is an immediate threat to your safety or the safety of others.

Therapy itself does not typically have any harmful side effects. However, certain therapeutic techniques or approaches may evoke emotional discomfort temporarily as you address and process challenging emotions or experiences. It's important to communicate openly with your therapist about any concerns or discomfort you may experience during the process.

Therapy aims to equip you with various coping mechanisms and strategies tailored to your needs. These may include relaxation techniques, mindfulness exercises, journaling, self-care practices, and challenging negative thought patterns. Your therapist can help you explore and develop personalized strategies to support your well-being between sessions.
